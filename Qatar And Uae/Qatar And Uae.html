Q A U

Qatar and the UAE (United Arab Emirates) are both Gulf countries in the Middle East, known for their wealth, modern infrastructure, and strategic locations.



  • Qatar


DOHA.QATAR

Doha is the capital city of Qatar, located on the northeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula. It is a modern and rapidly growing city known for its futuristic skyscrapers, luxurious lifestyle, and cultural heritage. Doha hosts iconic landmarks like the Museum of Islamic Art, designed by architect I.M. Pei, and Souq Waqif, a traditional market where visitors can experience Qatari culture, food, and handicrafts. The city also features the beautiful Corniche, a waterfront promenade offering stunning views of the skyline and the Arabian Gulf. Doha has become a global hub for sports, business, and tourism, highlighted by hosting the 2022 FIFA World Cup.

  • Uae


DUBAI.UAE

Dubai is a vibrant and modern city in the United Arab Emirates, located on the southeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula along the Persian Gulf. Known for its luxurious lifestyle, innovative architecture, and bustling economy, Dubai is a global hub for tourism, trade, and business. The city is famous for landmarks like the Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world, and the Palm Jumeirah, a man-made island shaped like a palm tree. Dubai also offers shopping experiences in its extravagant malls, traditional souks, and entertainment destinations such as the Dubai Fountain and Dubai Marina. With a mix of traditional Emirati culture and futuristic development, Dubai attracts millions of visitors each year, making it one of the most iconic cities in the world.
